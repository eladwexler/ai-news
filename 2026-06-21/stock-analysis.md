### Key Risks

**NVDA** — Rubin CPX demand could disappoint if hyperscalers slow capex; antitrust scrutiny of bundled CUDA ecosystem accelerating.

**MU** — HBM4 ramp yield issues could allow Samsung a second chance; NAND price spike may reverse if AI inference storage demand slows.

**ORCL** — Revenue concentration in one customer (OpenAI) is extreme; if OpenAI builds its own cloud or switches to Azure/AWS, RPO becomes fictional.

**AMKR** — TSMC could vertically integrate packaging; Arizona construction delays are endemic to the entire CHIPS Act ecosystem.

**INTC** — Apple and Google TPU deals are unconfirmed and explicitly denied by Intel; stock could gap down hard on denial. 18A-P is in risk production, not volume production.

**COHR** — $50M is an LOI, not a finalized award; InP capacity buildout takes 18–24 months to show in revenue.

**CSCO** — Historical AI revenue base is small; NVIDIA could shift to direct network sales or Spectrum-X cannibalizes Cisco's margins.

**ETN** — AI datacenter power orders have long backlogs (18–36 months); stock already re-rated significantly on AI tailwind over the past 2 years.

**AMD** — Bear thesis assumes NVDA ecosystem lock-in holds; any CUDA openness or hyperscaler custom-silicon substitution changes the picture.

**TXN** — A genuine automotive/industrial recovery (even from a low base) would cause a sharp short squeeze in the stock.

### Top Pick

**MU** is the single most interesting idea: it is the only US-listed company at the intersection of the two dominant bottlenecks this cycle — HBM (now 63% of AI chip costs, fastest-growing component) and NOR/SLC NAND (prices doubled in H1 2026) — with Samsung's credibility eroding and no non-Korean alternative for HBM production at scale.
